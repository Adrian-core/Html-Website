Classic Empanada

Here's a detailed recipe for making classic beef empanadas. This recipe combines a homemade dough with a savory beef filling, common in various cuisines but with a special focus on Latin American flavors.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)

For the Beef Filling:
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup green olives, chopped
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
Instructions:
Prepare the Dough:
  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Add Butter: Incorporate the cold, cubed butter into the flour mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Add Egg and Water: Beat the egg lightly and mix it into the flour mixture. Gradually add water and knead until a smooth dough forms.
    You may need a little more or less water, so add it slowly until the dough comes together well.
  4. Chill the Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

Make the Beef Filling:
  1. Cook Onion and Garlic: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until they are soft and translucent.
  2. Brown the Beef: Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spoon. Cook until browned.
  3. Add Spices and Liquids: Stir in the cumin, paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Add the beef broth and tomato sauce, simmering the mixture until most of the liquid has evaporated.
  4. Add Olives and Optional Ingredients: Stir in the chopped olives, and if using, the hard-boiled eggs and raisins. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

  2. Roll Out Dough: On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/8 inch thickness. Cut out round disc shapes using a large cookie cutter or a small bowl.
  3. Fill Empanadas: Place a spoonful of the beef mixture in the center of each dough round. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Use a fork to crimp the edges and seal the empanada.
  4. For Baking: Brush each empanada with beaten egg. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

Serve: Enjoy your beef empanadas hot, accompanied by salsa, guacamole, or your choice of dipping sauce.
