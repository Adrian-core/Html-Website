Italian Pasta Cabonara

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Italian dish known for its creamy sauce made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. Here's a basic recipe for you:
### Ingredients:

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti or your favorite pasta
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup (about 100g) grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1/2 cup (about 50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt, to taste
  • Olive oil (for cooking)
### Instructions:
  1. Cook the Pasta:
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    • Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente.
    • Reserve about 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.

  2. Prepare the Sauce:
    • - In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese. Set aside.
    • - Heat a bit of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta (or guanciale) and cook until it's crispy and golden brown. If using garlic, add it and saut√© until fragrant.

  3. Combine Pasta and Sauce:
    • - Add the drained pasta to the pan with the pancetta (or guanciale) and toss to coat.
    • - Remove the pan from heat, and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss the pasta until the sauce thickens and coats the noodles. If needed, add a bit of the reserved pasta water to achieve a creamy consistency.

  4. Season and Serve
    • Season the pasta with freshly ground black pepper. Be cautious with salt, as the pancetta and cheese are already salty.
    • Serve immediately, garnished with extra grated cheese and black pepper if desired.

    5. Enjoy your homemade Italian Pasta Carbonara! Feel free to customize it to your liking by adding more cheese, adjusting the level of pepper, or experimenting with different pasta shapes.
