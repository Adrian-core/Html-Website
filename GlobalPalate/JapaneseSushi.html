Here's a basic recipe for making Japanese sushi rolls, also known as "makizushi" or "maki rolls."
Ingredients:

  • Sushi rice (2 cups)
  • Nori seaweed sheets (4-6 sheets)
  • Sushi-grade fish (such as tuna, salmon, or shrimp) - about 1/2 lb (225g) total
  • Vegetables (such as cucumber, avocado, or carrot) - sliced into thin strips
  • Soy sauce, for serving(optional)
  • Wasabi, for serving(optional)
  • Pickled ginger, for serving(optional)

Equipment:
  • Bamboo sushi rolling mat (makisu)
  • Sharp knife

Instructions:
Prepare the Sushi Rice:
  1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Cook the rice according to the package instructions or using a rice cooker. Once cooked, let it cool slightly.
  3. In a small bowl, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Gently fold this mixture into the cooked rice until well combined. Allow the rice to cool completely before using.

Prepare the Fillings:
  1. Slice the sushi-grade fish and vegetables into thin strips. Ensure they are long enough to span the width of the nori sheet.

Assemble the Sushi Rolls:
  1. Place a sheet of nori shiny side down on the bamboo sushi rolling mat.
  2. Wet your hands with water to prevent the rice from sticking. Spread a thin layer of sushi rice evenly over the nori, leaving a small border along the top edge.
  3. Arrange the fish and vegetable fillings in a line across the center of the rice.
  4. Using the bamboo mat, roll the sushi tightly away from you, pressing gently to seal the roll.
  5. Wet the top border of the nori sheet with a little water to help seal the roll completely.
  6. Repeat the process with the remaining nori sheets and fillings.
Slice the Sushi Rolls:
  1. Use a sharp knife to slice each sushi roll into bite-sized pieces. Dip the knife in water between cuts to prevent sticking.
  2. Serve and Enjoy: Arrange the sushi rolls on a platter and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side.

Feel free to get creative with your sushi roll fillings and toppings. You can also experiment with different sauces, such as spicy mayo or eel sauce, for added flavor. Enjoy your homemade Japanese sushi rolls!
